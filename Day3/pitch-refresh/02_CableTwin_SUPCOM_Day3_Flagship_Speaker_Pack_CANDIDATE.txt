EXACT FOUR-MINUTE SCRIPT
======================================================================

SLIDE 1 — 0:00–0:20 — HOOK

Good afternoon. In our synthetic Tunisian cable-factory scenario, grid
power disappears for 58 minutes while a medium-voltage cable order is in
production. Electricity returns; certainty does not. CableTwin compares
recovery options before the plant commits equipment, quality resources,
people and the customer schedule.

SLIDE 2 — 0:20–0:50 — PROBLEM

Why is this difficult? The line sees the stop. Energy sees a restart
envelope. Quality sees incomplete process history. Logistics sees a truck
at 06:30. One 24-kilometre order now has three truths: drums one to six
remain outside the incident, drum seven is entirely on hold, and drum
eight is not started and must be replanned. Restarting the line is not
the same as recovering the operation.

SLIDE 3 — 0:50–1:20 — VALUE + TECHNICAL LOGIC

CableTwin is a local, read-only decision layer. Explainable ML retrieves
comparable synthetic cases and shows distance, differences and
out-of-distribution warning. The deterministic twin tests recovery
futures across energy, process, material, people, quality and delivery.
Hard rules reject impossible sequences. CableTwin never commands a PLC
and never releases cable: Quality, the laboratory and accountable humans
keep authority.

SLIDE 4 — 1:20–1:50 — DEMO

LIVE WORDING — USE ONLY AFTER RUNTIME ACCEPT:

Here is the same scenario in Jury mode. I inject the 58-minute outage:
drum seven stays on hold and the system retrieves comparable cases. I
reduce available restart power: no plan is feasible. I restore the
operator-entered limit: staged Plan C returns under visible assumptions.
The laboratory gate remains closed. Same engine, same rules, faster
replay—not a scripted shortcut.

PRE-ACCEPT FALLBACK:

This is the thirty-second acceptance contract now under review: incident,
hold state, comparable cases, a live no-feasible-plan stress test, then a
staged recovery that still leaves the laboratory gate closed. Until the
same-engine and browser gates pass, we present it as a specification, not
as a live capability.

SLIDE 5 — 1:50–2:15 — BUSINESS MODEL

We enter commercially in four disciplined steps: a paid data-and-decision
diagnostic, a read-only shadow pilot, a license per line or site with
configuration and support, then replication only after measured pilot
evidence. Pricing and ROI are pilot questions. We sell decision speed,
constraint visibility and traceability—not guaranteed savings.

SLIDE 6 — 2:15–2:40 — GO-TO-MARKET

The first pilot is one cable line for four to six weeks. We map the data
contract and hard rules, replay historical incidents plus synthetic
stress cases, compare CableTwin options with actual plant decisions, then
go, revise or stop. Success measures are evaluation time, violations
caught before action, stability, usability and traceability.

SLIDE 7 — 2:40–3:05 — COMPETITION

We complement existing systems; we replace none. SCADA and PLC control.
MES executes and records. APS plans capacity. QMS and laboratories test
and release product. CableTwin is the cross-domain, read-only decision
layer that makes incident alternatives, hard constraints, provenance and
human gates visible before the plant acts.

SLIDE 8 — 3:05–3:25 — TEAM + PROGRESS

I am Oussama Akir, a solo SUP'COM participant. During this hackathon I
built the public CableTwin demonstrator, its bounded planner, local ML
guidance, digital-twin views, verification and evidence package. I also
kept the product's limits visible. The next team extension is not a
fictional org chart; it is one real cable plant and its Quality authority.

SLIDE 9 — 3:25–3:40 — COMMERCIAL TRUTH

Today the honest numbers are zero signed pilots, zero revenue and zero
ROI claims. What is proven is a public working product, nine planner and
workflow checks, five ML checks, and a local, offline, read-only posture.
Synthetic evidence is not customer evidence.

SLIDE 10 — 3:40–4:00 — ASK + CLOSE

Our ask is precise: select CableTwin and connect one Tunisian cable-plant
design partner for a read-only shadow pilot. A power interruption lasts
58 minutes; industrial uncertainty can last for hours. CableTwin
retrieves the past, compares futures, blocks the impossible—and leaves
authorization to humans and the laboratory.

======================================================================
FOUR-MINUTE ADVERSARIAL Q&A
======================================================================

Q1 — Is the solution genuinely functional or only a scenario?
A — The current public demonstrator is functional, deterministic and
read-only. The factory data and incident are synthetic. It is not an
industrial qualification, a certified safety system or machine control.

Q2 — Where is the AI if the cases are synthetic?
A — The ML demonstrates a transparent retrieval-and-ranking mechanism:
neighbors, distance, differences and OOD warning. A pilot must recalibrate
and benchmark it on the plant's real historical incidents. It never
predicts conformity.

Q3 — How does CableTwin know which cable length is affected?
A — It reconstructs an investigation envelope from position, time and
encoded process assumptions. It does not locate a certain defect. The
entire seventh drum remains HOLD until tests and Quality release.

Q4 — Why say 24 km if only T7 and T8 are affected?
A — Twenty-four kilometres is the full synthetic customer order. T1–T6
are outside the incident; T7 carries quality uncertainty; T8 carries the
planning impact. We never claim to have saved 24 km.

Q5 — Who is accountable if CableTwin recommends the wrong plan?
A — CableTwin is consultative and read-only. Maintenance validates
restart readiness, Quality holds or releases product, the laboratory
provides evidence and the accountable plant manager signs the decision.

Q6 — Why can a 58-minute outage create hours of impact?
A — The synthetic delay includes safe stop, inspection, ordered restart
of auxiliaries, warm-up, stabilization, purge and controls. Every
duration must be calibrated on the pilot line; 58 minutes implies no
universal recovery duration.

Q7 — Why do MES, APS, QMS, SCADA or Excel not solve this?
A — They remain essential. CableTwin adds a bounded cross-domain what-if
layer for one incident decision: energy, process, quality, people,
material and delivery, with explicit provenance and human gates. It
steps aside where an incumbent already solves the need.

Q8 — What plant data does the pilot need?
A — Line timestamps and states, energy meters, MES orders, quality
events, material availability and operator decisions. The first pilot is
read-only shadow mode with a planner-validated data contract.

Q9 — How do you control cybersecurity risk?
A — Local or edge deployment, network segmentation, least-privilege
read-only inputs and an audit journal. No PLC command and no cloud
dependency are required for the initial use case.

Q10 — What is the business model and promised ROI?
A — Paid diagnostic, bounded shadow pilot, then a per-line or per-site
license with calibration and support. We promise no ROI before a measured
baseline and pilot evidence.

Q11 — How does it scale from a fictional line to many plants?
A — The branch engine can be reusable, but constraints, data mappings,
quality procedures and retrieval behavior are configured and validated
per site. We do not claim a universal once-trained model.

Q12 — Is the product useful only during Tunisian power cuts?
A — No. The current Tunisian context makes the problem concrete. The
same decision layer applies to site power limits, generator constraints,
machine stops, material shortages and other industrial disruptions.

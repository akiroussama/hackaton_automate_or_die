# CableTwin

## Updated concept note and Business Model Canvas

**Team:** SUPCOM

### Executive proposition

When a production line stops, CableTwin turns one urgent scheduling problem
into three constraint-feasible recovery routes - Service, Cost and Stability -
shows their consequences inside a live isometric workshop twin, lets a separate
local learned model suggest one existing route, and keeps approval with the
production manager.

CableTwin is a decision-support layer. It does not control machines, write to an
ERP/MES/APS, call an external AI service or use real factory data in the
demonstration.

## Problem and current Day 2 solution

The canonical synthetic scenario represents a fictional Tunisian cable factory
with three lines and ten orders. Line 2 stops from 10:00 to 14:00. Keeping every
order on its original line projects 620 minutes of cumulative delay, 180 minutes
of overtime, seven of ten orders on time and 188 km completed by 18:00.

The working Day 2 product connects:

1. an interactive isometric workshop twin and state-reactive synthetic telemetry;
2. a deterministic constraint planner that builds three recovery routes;
3. a separate local softmax-regression recommender that ranks those existing routes;
4. a separate exhaustive bounded verifier for the encoded demonstration;
5. explicit human preview, approval and audit.

## Technical separation and evidence

**Deterministic planner.** Builds Service, Cost and Stability schedules under
line eligibility, duration, downtime, non-overlap and horizon constraints. It
does not learn. The planner and workflow suite passes 9/9 checks.

**Separate local learned recommender.** Evaluates three already-built plans
through 16 explainable features. It was trained on 687 simulated incidents
generated by the twin itself. It reaches 95.7% training accuracy and 93.6%
accuracy on a held-out synthetic test subset. On the canonical incident, it
suggests Cost with approximately 79% displayed confidence. It never creates or
modifies a schedule. Its separate suite passes 5/5 checks.

**Separate exhaustive bounded verifier.** Enumerates 17,856
assignment-and-sequence candidates, finds 10,440 feasible complete schedules
and confirms three unique policy optima for this bounded demonstration.

**Human authority.** The manager may choose a route different from the learned
suggestion. The product records the approved route. No action is sent to a
machine or production system.

## Pilot and responsible deployment path

A first 4-to-6-week shadow pilot stays read-only in one workshop. It maps
planner-validated constraints, replays representative historical incidents and
compares CableTwin's alternatives with actual decisions using agreed KPIs. No
plan is written back and no machine is controlled.

The local learned recommender would be retrained only after the site has
sufficient representative real incident history. Training and inference remain
on-site, plant data does not leave the site, and evaluation uses a separate
representative holdout. If the learned layer is unavailable or underperforms,
the deterministic three-route planner remains the operational fallback.

Pilot evidence targets include assessment time, total delay, shift-end output,
on-time rate, overtime, line moves, validated-constraint violations, planner
usability and decision traceability. They are validation measures, not promised
ROI.
